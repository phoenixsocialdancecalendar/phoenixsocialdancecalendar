  <header class="about-hero">
    <p class="eyebrow">About this project</p>
    <h1>One place to check the Phoenix social dance scene.</h1>
    <p class="about-lead">
      Phoenix Social Dance Calendar brings together public dance listings from around Phoenix, Tempe, Mesa, Scottsdale, Chandler, Glendale, and nearby cities so it is easier to see what is happening without bouncing across a long list of separate websites and calendars.
    </p>
    <p class="about-disclaimer">
      This site was created for personal use to help aggregate dance events shared across social channels. It is not intended for business or professional purposes.
    </p>
  </header>

  <section class="about-grid" aria-label="About details">
    <article class="about-card">
      <h2>What It Is</h2>
      <p>
        This is a community-friendly guide, not an official calendar for any organizer, studio, venue, or promoter.
      </p>
      <p>
        The goal is simple: make it easier to scan what is happening in one place and click through to the original source when you want to confirm details.
      </p>
      <p>
        The focus is primarily on social dance events and social dance classes, plus some solo classes that are closely tied to a social dance tradition, such as salsa shines or similar style-specific training that clearly supports social dancing.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="about-card">
      <h2>How It Works</h2>
      <p>
        Most events on this site are gathered automatically from public event pages, calendars, feeds, and other publicly available listings, then normalized into one calendar view.
      </p>
      <p>
        Some listings may also be added or corrected manually when a source is image-only, hard to parse, or needs cleanup.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="about-card">
      <h2>How To Use It</h2>
      <ul class="about-list">
        <li>Browse the calendar by date.</li>
        <li>Use filters to narrow by dance family, specific style, event type, or source.</li>
        <li>Open the source link on any event when you want to verify the latest details directly.</li>
      </ul>
    </article>

    <article class="about-card about-card-warm">
      <h2>Reporting Problems Or Changes</h2>
      <p>
        If an event is incorrect, missing, duplicated, or should be removed, please email <a href="mailto:phoenixsocialdancecalendar@gmail.com">phoenixsocialdancecalendar@gmail.com</a>.
      </p>
      <p>
        If you run a venue, studio, or event and want your listings included, you can email that address as well.
      </p>
    </article>
  </section>

  <section class="about-section">
    <div class="about-section-heading">
      <p class="eyebrow">Important note</p>
      <h2>Why a listing may be wrong sometimes</h2>
    </div>
    <div class="about-panel">
      <p>
        Because this site pulls from public data that can change quickly, it can occasionally be incomplete, outdated, or incorrect.
      </p>
      <ul class="about-list">
        <li>An organizer changes the time, location, price, or event format after the listing was first published.</li>
        <li>An event gets canceled or moved on short notice.</li>
        <li>A source website is incomplete, hard to parse, or not fully machine-readable.</li>
        <li>Some events are not included at all because the details are only posted in places that are harder to aggregate reliably, such as Instagram posts, stories, flyers, or scattered social media updates instead of stable website listings.</li>
        <li>Different sites describe the same event differently, which can lead to missing details or imperfect categorization.</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="about-note">
        Treat this site as a strong starting point, especially for browsing, and check the original source before making plans for one-time or special events.
      </p>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section class="about-section">
    <div class="about-section-heading">
      <p class="eyebrow">Current coverage</p>
      <h2>Dance family structure</h2>
    </div>
    <div class="about-panel">
      <p>
        To make the calendar easier to scan, styles are grouped into a small set of dance families.
      </p>
      <ul class="about-list">
        <li>Traditional Swing: Swing, Lindy Hop, Balboa, Charleston, Solo Jazz, Blues, Swing / Blues</li>
        <li>West Coast Swing: West Coast Swing, WCS</li>
        <li>Latin: Salsa, Bachata, Salsa / Bachata, Kizomba, Zouk, Brazilian Zouk, Cha Cha, Argentine Tango, and most Tango listings unless they are clearly ballroom-oriented</li>
        <li>Ballroom: Ballroom, Hustle, and Tango when the listing is clearly framed in a ballroom context</li>
        <li>Country: Country, Line Dance, Two-Step, Country Swing</li>
        <li>Contra-Folk: Contra, English Country, Scottish Country</li>
        <li>Other: Social Dance and anything that does not fit cleanly elsewhere</li>
      </ul>
      <p>
        This structure exists to help with browsing and filtering, not to make a definitive statement about dance history, lineage, or identity.
      </p>
      <p>
        It is a best estimate based on how events are commonly presented in Phoenix-area listings, but it is not comprehensive. The anthropology of dance is complex, regional, and often contested, while this site uses a deliberately simple structure so the calendar stays usable.
      </p>
      <p>
        That means the categorization can sometimes be inaccurate or overly blunt. For example, a listing might use "tango" where a dancer would prefer a clearer distinction between ballroom tango and Argentine tango, or it might group East Coast Swing and Lindy Hop more loosely than some dancers would.
      </p>
      <p class="about-note">
        The goal is to be transparent about those tradeoffs. If a style is grouped poorly, we are very open to feedback and updates that improve the categorization over time.
      </p>
    </div>
  </section>
